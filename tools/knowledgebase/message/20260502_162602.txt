Based on the precise hardware state you provided, **(d) is your primary culprit, highly likely combined with a misunderstanding of (e) or the OAM disable bit.**

Here is exactly what is happening in the GBA PPU and why your emulator is displaying a kaleidoscope of "GAME BOY" texts while mGBA looks perfectly clean.

### 1. The Smoking Gun: WINOUT and the OBJ Window Mask (Hypothesis D)
You noted: `WINOUT = 0x3F27` and `DISPCNT` bit 15 is active. 

Let's break down `WINOUT = 0x3F27` exactly as the GBA hardware reads it:
*   **Outside Window (Bits 0-5) = `0x27` (Binary `0010 0111`)**
    *   BG0=1, BG1=1, BG2=1, BG3=0, **OBJ=0**, Color Special=1
*   **Inside OBJ Window (Bits 8-13) = `0x3F` (Binary `0011 1111`)**
    *   BG0=1, BG1=1, BG2=1, BG3=1, **OBJ=1**, Color Special=1

You asked: *"even though my OBJ Window mask covers ~3985 pixels (10% of screen), OBJ should be visible inside that mask only?"*

**Absolutely, YES.** Because bit 4 of `WINOUT` is `0`, the OBJ layer is **strictly disabled** for any pixel on the screen that does not fall underneath an active OBJ Window mask pixel. 

Many test ROMs (like the `jsmolka` tests or open-source BIOS clones) intentionally leave an absolute mess of sprites in OAM specifically to test if emulator developers have implemented Windowing layer-gating correctly. If your compositing logic is drawing those 110 sprites *outside* that 10% mask, your windowing implementation for the OBJ layer is failing, and you are seeing garbage that the ROM author intentionally left there.

### 2. The "Non-Disabled" Sprites Trap (Hypotheses B & C)
You mentioned that 128 OAM entries are "non-disabled". You must be incredibly careful about how you read the Disable bit in GBA OAM. 

In `ATTR0`:
*   **Bit 8** is the Affine (Rotation/Scaling) Flag.
*   **Bit 9** changes its meaning based on Bit 8!
    *   If `Bit 8 == 0`, Bit 9 is the **Disable Flag**.
    *   If `Bit 8 == 1`, Bit 9 is the **Double-Size Flag** (and the sprite is ENABLED).

If your emulator checks `if (attr0 & 0x0200)` to hide sprites without first checking if it's an affine sprite (`attr0 & 0x0100`), you will incorrectly hide affine double-size sprites, OR you will fail to hide sprites that the hardware considers disabled. 

The correct check for a disabled sprite is:
`bool disabled = (attr0 & 0x0300) == 0x0200;`

### 3. The Affine Zero-Matrix Collapse (Hypothesis E)
If those 110 sprites truly are enabled (perhaps they are Affine, so Bit 9 just means Double-Size), why does mGBA not show them even if Windowing was turned off? 

Look at the Affine Matrices (`PA, PB, PC, PD`) for those extra sprites. 
The GBA maps screen pixels to texture pixels using direct multiplication, not division:
`texture_x = PA * screen_dx + PB * screen_dy`

If the ROM leaves unused affine sprites with `PA=0, PB=0, PC=0, PD=0`, then for every single screen pixel in the bounding box, `texture_x` and `texture_y` will equal `0`. The entire sprite collapses to sample the exact single pixel at `(0,0)` of its texture. If that pixel is transparent, the entire 64x64 bounding box becomes completely invisible.

**A common emulator bug:** Many devs see an all-zero matrix and write a safety check: `if (PA==0 && PD==0) { PA=256; PD=256; }` to prevent division-by-zero or math errors. If you do this, you will artificially force 100 collapsed, invisible sprites to suddenly render at a scale of 1.0! The GBA hardware has no division here; it happily multiplies by zero and collapses the sprite.

### How to Diagnose this right now:

1.  **Fix / Verify your Window Logic:** Hardcode a check in your final compositing loop: if a pixel is outside the OBJ window mask, force the OBJ layer's alpha/color value to 0 for that pixel. See if the overlapping texts instantly vanish.
2.  **Open mGBA's OAM Viewer:**
    *   Pause at 2.5 seconds.
    *   Look at those 110 "normal" sprites.
    *   Look at their **Y coordinate**. Are they set to `160` or higher? (The GBA screen is 160 pixels high. Sprites with `160 <= Y <= 255` are off-screen unless they wrap, which Affine sprites do not).
    *   Look at their **Matrix**. Are `PA, PB, PC, PD` all `0000`? 
    *   Look at their **OBJ Mode**. Are they genuinely normal (Mode 0), or did you misparse `ATTR0` bits 10-11?
3.  **Check your Disable Logic:** Ensure your code strictly uses `(attr0 & 0x0300) == 0x0200` to define a disabled sprite.

**Conclusion:** The overlapping text is almost certainly caused by your emulator ignoring `WINOUT` bit 4 and rendering hidden OBJ pixels outside the OBJ Window mask, combined with potentially rendering zero-matrix affine sprites that hardware natively collapses into transparency.
